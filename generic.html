My Interest in IT

Where it all began

According to my mum, my interest in IT started from the moment I learnt what buttons were. I loved to push button so much she tells me, that one day when I was about 5 I managed to push the silent alarm button at a bank and cause a whole squad of police to come around and "embarrass her to death". The first time that I remember falling in love with IT was when my grandmother purchased her first computer. I helped her set it up and taught her how to use it. It was a gigantic machine that weight as much as a fat 5 year old, would take an eternity to turn on and loaded images one line at a time. My grandmother was a very creative lady and bought the computer so she could download knitting patterns and design new embroidery templates. Not long from there I got my first phone, a black and white NOKIA, that wasn't good for anything other than playing snake and taking calls for a few minutes. Then my first family computer, first Gameboy, laptop, iPad, PlayStation and so on. I don't have extensive professional experience in IT but have been involved in hardware setup, basic IT support and have worked with developers to create new healthcare systems for the aged and disability sectors.
